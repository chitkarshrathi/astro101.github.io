The Earth

Mass - 5.97219 x 10 24 kilograms

Our Earth is just a small speck in the vastness of the universe.

Here it's just 5 pixels in height and width. Just barely enough to be seen

on the screen.

The Sun

Mass - 3.955 x 10 30 kilograms or 1 Solar Mass

This is our Sun, the center of our solar system. It's massive compared to

the Earth, about 333000 thousand times our mass. But even it falls short

compared to the rest of the universe.

A Stellar-Mass Black Hole

Mass - About 3 to 100 Solar Masses

This is a black hole, a region of space where gravity is so strong that

nothing can escape, not even light. Even though a Stellar-Mass Black

Holes Mass can range from 3 to 100 Solar Masses, our example is about

10 times the size of the Sun, so 10 Solar Masses.

An Intermediate Mass Black Hole

Mass - 100 to 100000 Solar Masses

This is an Intermediate Mass Black Hole. It's about 1000 times the mass

of our Sun. Intermediate Black Holes aren't commonly found, since we

ony have one example that has been found so far.

A SUPERMASSIVE BLACK HOLE



This is as big as it gets. A supermassive black hole is bigger than 100000 solar masses.

Our example is 1000000000 times the mass of the Sun.

Our Earth would not even be visible in front of this. The sun would be smaller than a pixel.
